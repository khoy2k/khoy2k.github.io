IREC Rocket Design & Fabrication

May 2024 – June 2024

OpenRocket MATLAB Machining Airframe Fabrication

A rapid-cycle sounding rocket project bridging theoretical aerodynamic stability analysis with the physical fabrication of composite and polymer airframe components.

The Engineering Challenge

Designing a competition-ready launch vehicle requires strict adherence to target apogees and safety margins. The primary engineering challenge was simulating and predicting how slight changes in mass distribution across the payload, recovery, and motor configurations would impact the aerodynamic stability of the rocket at high velocities.

Furthermore, this project required translating digital aerodynamic simulations into physical hardware with strict tolerances, demanding a direct transition from computational models to hands-on machine shop fabrication.

Aerodynamic Analysis & Simulation

To ensure a stable flight profile and verify our target apogee, I ran iterative simulations using OpenRocket. I analyzed the relationship between the Center of Pressure (CP) and Center of Gravity (CG) across various motor delays and payload weights.

To validate the OpenRocket outputs, I utilized MATLAB to model the vehicle's static margin sensitivity based on varying airframe dimensions.

Static Stability Margin Calculation (MATLAB):


% Calculate Static Margin for IREC Launch Vehicle
% Ensure CG is ahead of CP by at least 1.5 to 2.0 calibers

body_diameter = 4.0; % inches
cg_location = 48.5; % inches from nose tip
cp_location = 56.2; % inches from nose tip

% Calculate stability caliber
static_margin = (cp_location - cg_location) / body_diameter;

fprintf('Calculated Static Margin: %.2f calibers\n', static_margin);

if static_margin >= 1.5 && static_margin <= 2.5
    disp('Status: STABLE. Vehicle is within safe flight parameters.');
else
    disp('Status: UNSTABLE. Adjust mass distribution or fin geometry.');
end

Fabrication & Hardware Integration

With the aerodynamic profile verified, I transitioned to the shop floor to manufacture the flight hardware. Utilizing precision machining techniques, I turned a custom Delrin nose cone on the lathe to match the required ogive profile, minimizing aerodynamic drag.

I also led the fabrication and assembly of the PVC airframe, ensuring the motor mount and centering rings were structurally sound enough to withstand maximum dynamic pressure (Max-Q) and the shock of parachute deployment.
