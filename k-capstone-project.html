Autonomous Patrol Robot Systems Analysis

Dec 2025 – Mar 2026

Systems Engineering Python (Pandas) MATLAB Trade Studies

A comprehensive systems engineering trade study evaluating the feasibility of deploying autonomous security robots, backed by a custom multi-criteria evaluation model.

The Client & The Problem

This consulting-style engineering project was developed during the K-Capstone Challenge, hosted by the Consulate General of the Republic of Korea in Seattle. Our team was assigned to work with our industry partner, DOGU, to solve a core operational challenge.

DOGU needed a rigorous, data-backed recommendation on whether their indoor/outdoor autonomous patrol robots were viable, safe, and acceptable for deployment in complex public facilities. The evaluation had to account for strict operational constraints, including public privacy concerns, navigation robustness, and the impact on human operational workload.

Systems Engineering Framework

To provide a structured recommendation, I looked beyond basic hardware specifications and built a quantitative index model based on four core pillars: Need, Demand, Acceptance, and Concern.

To ground this evaluation in established academic theory, I utilized the UTAUT (Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology) framework. This allowed us to translate highly subjective stakeholder feelings into objective engineering metrics that could be scored and plotted.

Data Processing & Modeling

We gathered qualitative survey data from over 100 respondents. To evaluate this data efficiently, I utilized Python (Pandas) to automate the preprocessing pipeline, clean the dataset, and apply our weighted scoring matrix.

Automated Scoring Logic (Python/Pandas):


import pandas as pd
import numpy as np

def calculate_viability_score(df, weights):
    """
    Applies the UTAUT-based weighted matrix to survey respondent data.
    Weights dictate the importance of Need, Demand, Acceptance, and Concern.
    """
    # Normalize survey responses (1-5 Likert scale to 0-1)
    metrics = ['need_score', 'demand_score', 'acceptance_score', 'concern_score']
    for metric in metrics:
        df[f'{metric}_norm'] = (df[metric] - 1) / 4.0

    # Invert the 'concern' score since high concern negatively impacts viability
    df['concern_score_norm'] = 1.0 - df['concern_score_norm']

    # Calculate final weighted index
    df['final_viability_index'] = (
        (df['need_score_norm'] * weights['need']) +
        (df['demand_score_norm'] * weights['demand']) +
        (df['acceptance_score_norm'] * weights['acceptance']) +
        (df['concern_score_norm'] * weights['concern'])
    )
    
    return df.sort_values(by='final_viability_index', ascending=False)

Final Output & Trade Study Results

The automated scoring model allowed us to generate a definitive decision matrix using MATLAB. By cross-referencing the viability index against specific facility constraints, we delivered a comprehensive feasibility report to DOGU.

The project successfully demonstrated how to bridge the gap between human stakeholder requirements and technical hardware deployment, resulting in a data-backed Concept of Operations strategy for autonomous patrol robots.
