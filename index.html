Multimodal EMG-Controlled FPV Drone

 Jan 2026 – Present

Building a hands-free UAV control testbed that maps EMG and voice commands into flight-controller inputs while preserving manual override capability.

Overview: Built an FPV drone testbed for a multimodal UAV control project, including avionics integration, wiring, hardware assembly, and communication between a Raspberry Pi, ESP32, and Betaflight-configured flight controller.

Problem & Approach

Traditional RC controllers require full manual engagement. I configured separate UART channels so the conventional RC receiver can remain available for manual override while an ESP32 relays decoded EMG and voice command signals from the Raspberry Pi into the flight controller.

Outcome

Currently validating the end-to-end signal path, troubleshooting power integrity, and performing flight readiness checks for safe test operations.

Python Raspberry Pi ESP32 Betaflight

Autonomous Patrol Robot Systems Analysis

High Power Rocket - NAR Level 1 Certification

 Mar 2024 – Apr 2024

Designed, built, and successfully recovered a high-power rocket to achieve National Association of Rocketry (NAR) Level 1 Certification.

Overview: Serving as a practical foundation in launch vehicle mechanics and recovery systems, I led a design project for the STEM Rocketry Team at Everett Community College to build and launch a high-power amateur rocket.

Problem & Approach

A primary challenge during development was troubleshooting stability issues for a three-meter vehicle. I utilized simulation tools to verify the center of gravity (CG) and center of pressure (CP) relationship, adjusted the mass distribution, and applied hands-on fabrication techniques to assemble the airframe, motor mount, and parachute recovery system.

Outcome

Successfully launched and recovered the vehicle intact on an H/I class motor, officially earning my NAR Level 1 Certification and validating the aerodynamic stability corrections in a real-world flight.
