About Me

I am an aerospace engineering student at the University of Washington pursuing a B.A. in Aeronautics & Astronautics with a minor in Informatics. My engineering philosophy is straightforward: the best designs come from a fundamental understanding of how systems are built, integrated, and maintained.

Before diving into advanced flight mechanics and systems engineering at UW, I completed a two-year Aviation Maintenance Technician program to earn my FAA Airframe & Powerplant (A&P) certification. While my focus is strictly on engineering design and research, this hands-on training profoundly shapes my approach. It ensures that the systems I design in SolidWorks or simulate in MATLAB are not just theoretically sound, but practically manufacturable, reliable, and logical to assemble.

Currently, I am focused on flight systems, UAV control interfaces, and robotics. Whether I am tuning a flight controller for a multimodal drone or structuring a trade study for autonomous patrol robots, I thrive at the intersection of mechanical hardware, software control, and systems-level thinking.

Engineering Projects

Multimodal Voice-EMG Control for UAVs

Feb 2026 – Present

Developing a hands-free UAV control interface integrating electromyography (EMG) and voice inputs for reliable teleoperation.

Overview: As part of an undergraduate technical project, I am engineering a multimodal control system that maps physiological and auditory inputs to drone flight mechanics. The core challenge involves translating complex, noisy human inputs into stable, low-latency control commands.

Problem & Approach

Traditional RC controllers require full manual engagement. I integrated EMG and gesture-related inputs with voice commands, bridging the hardware/software gap by mapping these signals into a Betaflight-based flight controller, focusing heavily on control reliability and latency reduction.

Outcome

Currently evaluating prototype reliability against false activations and assessing Human-Machine Interface (HMI) efficiency during simulated flight tasks.

C++ Betaflight Hardware Integration Control Systems

Autonomous Patrol Robot Systems Analysis

Dec 2025 – Present

Conducted a comprehensive systems engineering trade study for the deployment of autonomous security robots for the Consulate General of the Republic of Korea.

Overview: A consulting-style engineering project translating high-level stakeholder needs into strict operational requirements, evaluating the feasibility of deploying autonomous patrol robots in dynamic environments.

Problem & Approach

The sponsor needed a rigorous, data-backed recommendation. I defined mission objectives and Concepts of Operations (ConOps), then developed a weighted decision matrix utilizing Python (Pandas) and direct stakeholder interviews to evaluate navigation robustness and operational workload.

Outcome

Delivered a comprehensive feasibility report and deployment matrix, providing a structured evaluation of robot capabilities against facility constraints.

Systems Engineering Python (Pandas) Trade Studies ConOps

IREC Rocket Design & Fabrication

May 2024 – June 2024

Analyzed flight stability and fabricated composite/polymer airframe components for a competitive sounding rocket.

Overview: A rapid-cycle project bridging aerospace fundamentals with raw manufacturing. Responsible for ensuring aerodynamic stability and fabricating critical structural components.

Problem & Approach

The vehicle required strict adherence to apogee targets and stability margins. I simulated payload configurations using OpenRocket, calculated CP/CG relationships, and transitioned to the shop floor to machine a Delrin nose cone and fabricate the PVC fuselage.

Outcome

Successfully manufactured flight hardware that matched simulated stability criteria, contributing to the team's competition readiness.
